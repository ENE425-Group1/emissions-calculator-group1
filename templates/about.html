Methodology

The methodology...

CO2 equivalents (CO2e) contain CO2, CH4 and N2O, i.e. the relevant greenhouse gases from the transport sector, multiplied with their respective 100-year Global Warming Potentials and summed up. The calculation of CO2e requires the calculation of the following input pollutants (these pollutants have to be calculated in the background): ▪ FC (fuel consumption in g/km) – required as input to calculate CO2 (reported) ▪ CO2 (reported) ▪ CH4 ▪ HC, since CH4 is derived from HC ▪ N2O A new parameter “GWP” for the 100-year global warming potential (GWP), required to calculate CO2 equivalents from CH4 and N2O, need to be included. HBEFA has set it to the following values according with IPCC 2007: ▪ CH4: 25 ▪ N2O: 298 The complete list of CO2e factors from IPCC 2007 can be found on this page: https://climatechangeconnection.org/emissions/co2-equivalents/ The CO2 emissions are calculated as “total CO2“ – based on the assumption that the carbon in the fuel is totally oxidised into CO2. Methane (CH4) emissions is taken from total HC. HC components in the HBEFA are differentiated in CH4, NMHC, Benzene, Toluene and Xylene. Their emissions are calculated by their share in total HC-emissions. The shares are differentiated by aggregated emission concept (vehicle category and fuel type) and emission category (hot, cold start, evaporation). For CNG the HBEFA assumes a CH4/HC fraction of 92% for LDV and HGVs. Further information on CH4/HC ratios can be found in the report below, specificly in table 8 on p.49. The report also gives further info on emissions of Nitrous oxide (N20). HBEFA Development report can be found here: https://www.hbefa.net/e/documents/HBEFA41_Development_Report.pdf Alternate methodology based on the COPERT calculations, which is quite similiar to the HBEFA methodology: https://www.emisia.com/utilities/copert-data/. COPERT does not consider different traffick situations, while HBEFA do.
